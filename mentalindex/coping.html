What is coping?

Coping is learning to tolerate distress without becoming overwhelmed.

It is important for everyone for managing emotions, dealing with unpleasant people and situations, and living through urges to engage in unhealthy behaviors of all kinds.

Basically, coping is living.

When we are in very hard situations that we cannot escape, we must learn to cope because searching for a way out is futile (and it will cause more distress).

Everyone copes every day, but it's important to learn new tools to cope even better.


Kinds of coping

  • Distraction: doing an activity

  • Self-soothing: using your senses to become calmer

  • Imagery: imagining that you are somewhere calm

  • Urge management: delaying when you want to engage in an unhealthy behavior

  • Worry list: writing a list of everything you're worried about.

  • Calming colors: looking at calming colors.

  • Uplifting quotes: looking at a curated selection of messages.

  • Cognitive reframing: identifying thought patterns that are causing you distress and replacing them with more logical or positive thoughts.
